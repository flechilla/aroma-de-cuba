# Cuban Rum: The Spirit of an Island

Some drinks transcend the simple act of drinking. **Cuban rum** is one of them: a spirit that tells stories of sugarcane plantations, colonial ports, and Havana nights where son music mixes with the clinking of glasses.

Cuba didn't invent rum, but it perfected it. And that perfection has a name: **Cuban light rum**, a style that revolutionized the world industry and remains the benchmark for distillers across the globe.

## History of Rum in Cuba

### Colonial Origins

The history of Cuban rum begins in the 16th century when Spanish colonizers brought sugarcane to the Caribbean. Molasses, a byproduct of sugar mills, was fermented to produce a rustic aguardiente consumed by slaves and sailors.

By the 18th century, Cuba was already exporting "sugarcane aguardiente" to Spain and other colonies. But the real qualitative leap came in the 19th century.

### The Light Rum Revolution

In 1862, a Spanish immigrant named **Facundo Bacard칤 Mass칩** experimented with filtering and aging techniques in Santiago de Cuba. The result was a smoother, cleaner, more elegant rum: the birth of **Cuban light rum**.

This innovation changed everything. While other Caribbean rums were heavy and harsh, Cuban rum seduced with its refinement. By 1900, Cuba was synonymous with world-class rum.

### The 20th Century and the Revolution

The 1959 revolution transformed the industry. Distilleries were nationalized and Bacard칤 went into exile (today producing in Puerto Rico and Mexico). The Cuban government created **Cuba Ron S.A.**, which groups the main national brands.

The Havana Club brand, which had belonged to the Arechabala family, was relaunched in 1972 and today is the international face of Cuban rum, distributed globally by Pernod Ricard.

## How to Taste Cuban Rum

### For Aged Rums (7+ years)

1. **Room temperature** - Never cold, you'll lose aromas
2. **Snifter or short glass** - Allows aromas to concentrate
3. **Observe the color** - From amber to mahogany indicates aging
4. **Smell first** - Before tasting, identify notes
5. **Small sips** - Let it coat your entire tongue
6. **A splash of water** (optional) - Opens new aromas
