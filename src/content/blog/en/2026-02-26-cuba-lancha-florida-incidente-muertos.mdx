A serious armed incident in Cuban territorial waters has resulted in four people dead and six wounded. According to the Ministry of the Interior (MININT), Border Guard troops intercepted a speedboat from Florida, United States, on Wednesday which allegedly opened fire against official units.

## Confrontation Details

The event took place on the island's northern coast, specifically near Corralillo, Villa Clara. According to the official version read on national television, the civil vessel did not obey the order to stop and responded with gunfire to the interception.

Cuban forces repelled the attack, resulting in the death of four crew members from the speedboat. Six other occupants were injured and evacuated for medical assistance. On the Cuban side, at least one officer was reported with minor injuries.

## International Reaction and Bilateral Tension

This incident occurs at a time of extreme tension between Havana and Washington, marked by the recrudescence of the energy blockade and delicate diplomatic negotiations.

*   **White House:** U.S. Vice President JD Vance confirmed that the American government is closely monitoring the situation and that an investigation has been launched in Florida.
*   **Ongoing Investigation:** Cuban authorities claim that weapons were recovered on board the vessel and that those detained are under judicial custody.

## FAQ

### Where exactly did the speedboat incident occur?
The confrontation took place north of the Villa Clara province, near Corralillo, within Cuban territorial waters.

### What is the nationality of the deceased?
Identities and specific nationalities have not been revealed yet, although it was confirmed that the vessel had Florida registration.

### What is the United States government saying about this event?
The Trump administration, through the White House, has expressed concern and is gathering information regarding the use of force by Cuban troops.

### How does this affect the current energy crisis?
While not directly related to power generation, the incident aggravates diplomatic isolation and tension at the island's maritime borders.
