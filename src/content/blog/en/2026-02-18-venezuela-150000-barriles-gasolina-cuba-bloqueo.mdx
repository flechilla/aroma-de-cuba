Amid the most severe oil blockade in decades, Venezuela is not giving up. A tanker that has transported Venezuelan fuel to Cuba since April 2025 finished loading **150,000 barrels of gasoline** destined for the island this week, according to a shipping schedule reviewed by Reuters.

## Trump's Energy Siege

Since early February 2026, the Trump administration intercepted tankers carrying Venezuelan crude and threatened tariffs against any nation that dares to supply Havana. The results: canceled flights, extended blackouts, industrial shutdowns, and the 2026 Habano Festival suspended.

## Caracas Pushes Back

Despite the risk of commercial retaliation, Venezuela has maintained a reduced supply line. The 150,000-barrel cargo covers barely three days of minimum consumption in Cuba, but sends a clear political signal: Caracas remains willing to defy Washington.
